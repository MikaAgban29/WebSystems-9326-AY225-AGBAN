About Bacoor City

History, Vision & Mission

Our History

Bacoor, a first-class component city in the province of Cavite, has a rich history dating back to the Spanish colonial period. Originally known as "Bakood," meaning fence or enclosure, the city played a significant role in the Philippine Revolution. It was here that the Battle of Zapote Bridge took place on February 17, 1897, marking a crucial victory for Filipino revolutionaries.

From its humble beginnings as a small fishing village, Bacoor has transformed into one of the most progressive cities in the Philippines. It was converted from a municipality to a component city through Republic Act No. 10160, which was ratified on June 23, 2012.

👁️

Vision

A progressive, inclusive, and sustainable city where every citizen enjoys quality life, accessible services, transparent governance, and equal opportunities for growth and development.

🎯

Mission

To deliver excellent public service through transparent, accountable, and participatory governance, while fostering economic growth, environmental sustainability, and social equity for all Bacoorenos.

Core Values

Integrity, Excellence, Accountability, Innovation, and Service - these values guide our commitment to building a better Bacoor for present and future generations.
